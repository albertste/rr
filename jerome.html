Bio

This is Jerome. He's from Paris and has lived in the US, Portugal, and Dubai. He started out working in the technology industry in California and now heads his family-run investment firm. He spends most of his time working, seeing friends, and staying in shape.

Q&A

Hey Jerome. Thanks for being on R&R. Why did you join?

When my friend Kayla asked me to join after my relationship ended, I brushed her off at first. I'd heard of the success she was having with R&R but didn't think it was for me.

But I gave it a second thought. It might help with the time-consuming aspects of dating. So I gave it a shot.

What does a typical day for you look like?

I usually dedicate my mornings to work and my afternoons to sports, friends, and family. I have the good fortune of being able to set my schedule. I travel from time to time for both work and leisure.

What are your hobbies?

I play padel, basketball, tennis. I do some weightlifting.

I also read a lot. A big part of my job is to keep on top of how the world really works so I can continue to make successful investments.

What are you looking for in terms of physique and personality?

Mostly chemistry. Physique is important in that it reflects her discipline and that she takes care of herself. Nobody wants to date a slob. But beauty inevitably fades away. Open-mindedness and authenticity matter too.

What would you do together if we find you a match?

After a period of getting to know each other, we'd travel together for a while. If our relationship comes out stronger, that's probably a good sign.

What age range are you looking for?

Younger than me. I find young people today to be more open-minded. There are many exceptions though so I'm flexible.

What's the hallmark of a good relationship?

Caring, laughter, communication.

How do you approach problems in a relationship?

Just sit down and talk things over. Get a good understanding of what you're doing and where you're going. Hug.

What are some items on your bucket list?

I don't really keep a bucket list. I have personal, career, and family goals.

What is trust, in your opinion?

Authenticity. Safety. Doing what you said you'll do.

What are your favorite topics for in-depth conversation?

I usually keep conversations light and fun. Nothing wrong with going deep every once in a while. Current events, psychology, relationships.

What's a snack that you like?

100% dark chocolate.

Three words your friends would use to describe you?

Savvy, smart, funny. Probably. I have nice friends.

What's your ideal Saturday night?

Going out with friends for dinner. Staying at home. Calling someone close.

What was your first ever job?

Product manager at Apple.

Would you consider your values more traditional or progressive?

My values are family, strong work ethic, integrity. I don't know or care what people might label them.

What are your favorite books?

Outwitting the Devil opened my eyes to how the world works. The Magic of Thinking Big taught me to aim high. The Dictator's Handbook taught me about politics and power. Flow taught me about happiness.

Do you have social media?

I don't. I use messengers to stay in touch with friends and email for work.
